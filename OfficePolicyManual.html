Section 2

The DBRE Brokerage System

The DBRE Brokerage System is the firm's proprietary operational framework. It provides Advisors with the infrastructure, tools, and support they need to operate as high-performing independent professionals — without building it themselves. The system rests on three pillars.

The Three Pillars
01
Infrastructure & Operations
Transaction coordination, document management, MLS entry support, and all back-office functions that allow Advisors to focus on clients rather than paperwork. Powered by the Advisor Boardroom and the Knowledge Vault.
02
Brand & Marketing
Access to DBRE's brand system — templates, Canva assets, letterhead, and marketing materials — enabling every Advisor to present at the firm's luxury standard without individual design investment.
03
Training & Standards
This policy manual, all SOPs, field guides, scripts, and transaction workflows. The complete DBRE playbook — so no Advisor ever has to figure out how to handle a situation alone.
Communication Channels
1
Advisor Boardroom
The primary document repository. All transaction files, executed agreements, policy documents, and SOPs live here. Advisors are required to upload all executed contracts and documents to the Boardroom per DBRE document retention standards.
2
Knowledge Vault
The firm's resource library. Templates, forms, field guides, scripts, sign-in sheet backups, and reference documents. If you need it, it is in the Knowledge Vault.
3
Teams Channels
Day-to-day communication, announcements, and collaboration. All Advisors are expected to maintain active presence in designated channels. Policy updates and firm announcements are distributed via Teams.
4
Message Center
Client-facing email templates and communication sequences. Advisors use Message Center templates as the starting point for all standard client communications — consultation confirmations, showing follow-ups, offer notifications, and closing touchpoints.

Section 5

MLS, Listing & Cooperative Compensation Standards

All listings must comply with Cape Fear Realtors® MLS rules and DBRE's internal listing standards. MLS entry is handled exclusively by the BIC or Operations Specialist — Advisors submit the completed MLS Information Sheet and do not enter listings directly.

MLS Entry Requirements
!
Entry deadline: 1 business day from marketing commencement
All listings must be entered into the MLS within 1 business day of marketing commencement per Clear Cooperation (DBRE-POL-002). Any prior reference to a 3-business-day deadline is superseded. Advisors must submit the MLS Information Sheet in time to meet this deadline.
2
MLS entry by BIC or Operations Specialist only
Advisors do not enter listings into the MLS directly. All entries, status changes, and price adjustments are made by the BIC or Operations Specialist. Unauthorized MLS entries are a policy violation subject to disciplinary action.
3
Coming Soon: 30-day maximum, no showings or offers
  • No showings or offers may be accepted during Coming Soon status
  • Coming Soon may not exceed 30 days before transitioning to Active
  • Pre-approval or proof of funds required before any showing after Active status is set
SOP Note: DBRE-OPS-004 and DBRE-OPS-005 currently reference a 3-business-day MLS entry deadline. This is an error pending correction. The operative deadline is 1 business day per DBRE-POL-002 in all cases.

Section 6

Open House Standards

Open houses are a client experience — not merely a marketing activity. Every DBRE open house must reflect the firm's luxury service standard from the moment visitors arrive. This section is a summary; the complete governing policy is DBRE-POL-011.

Required Steps
1
Submit Open House Request Form — minimum 48 hours in advance
The Open House Submission Form must be submitted at least 48 hours before the scheduled event. Coordinate with Carolyn Blue for sign pickup. Signs must be DBRE branded — no personal or third-party signage.
2
DBRE Presentation Standard — non-negotiable
  • Fresh flowers — required at every open house, no exceptions
  • Music: low-volume jazz or classical only — no religious music, no pop
  • Staging: light and tasteful — no heavy rearrangement without seller permission
  • Scent: neutral — no candles, sprays, or diffusers
  • Materials: DBRE Canva templates or brand-compliant self-created materials
3
Sign-in required — digital preferred
All visitors must sign in. Digital sign-in via tablet is preferred. Physical backup available in the Knowledge Vault. All records must be retained per DBRE document retention standards.
4
Safety requirements
  • Share your open house schedule with a colleague or the office prior to the event
  • Avoid being alone with a single unknown visitor if possible
  • See DBRE-POL-011 for the complete safety protocol
Dual Agency at Open Houses
If a visitor expresses interest in making an offer and you represent the seller: dual agency must be disclosed immediately. Notify the broker and obtain written consent from both parties before proceeding. Prior broker approval is not required — immediate notification is. Document the disclosure in writing.
Full policy: DBRE-POL-011 · Open House Policy & Procedures · v2.0

Buyer Representation Standards

No DBRE Advisor may show property to a buyer without a signed Exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement in place. This is non-negotiable — it reflects both DBRE policy and the post-NAR settlement standard of practice.

BRA Requirements
!
BRA required before any showing — no exceptions
The Exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement must be signed before any property is shown. Not after the first showing. Not at the consultation if a showing is scheduled before it. Before the showing. If a buyer is unwilling to sign a BRA, DBRE Advisors do not show property.
2
Standard BRA terms
  • Standard buyer rep fee: 2.5% (negotiable)
  • Standard term: 90 days
  • Form 220 must be executed before showing to document co-op compensation terms
  • Executed BRA uploaded to Advisor Boardroom within 1 business day of execution
Multiple Offer Protocol
Multiple offer situations require adherence to DBRE-POL-MOP-001 on both the buyer and seller side. The protocol covers step-by-step process, communication standards, scripts, and disclosure obligations.

Section 8

Professional Standards & Fair Housing

DBRE Advisors are expected to conduct themselves at the highest standard of professional practice at all times — in client interactions, in the MLS, in communications with cooperating brokers, and in the communities where they work.

Code of Conduct
1
NAR Code of Ethics compliance required
All DBRE Advisors are required to know and comply with the NAR Code of Ethics. Ethics violations are treated as policy violations and may result in termination of the ICA.
2
Fair housing — zero tolerance
DBRE has a zero-tolerance policy for any conduct that violates the Fair Housing Act or its NC equivalents. This includes steering, discriminatory advertising language, selective disclosure, or any differential treatment based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability, or any other protected class.
3
Professional communication standards
  • Negative commentary about other brokerages, Advisors, or firms is strictly prohibited in any written or verbal form
  • Disputes with cooperating parties are handled through proper channels — not social media or group chats
  • Social media posts involving DBRE, clients, or transactions must comply with DBRE-POL-007
4
Agency disclosure
Advisors must provide the Working with Real Estate Agents brochure and make proper agency disclosure at first substantial contact with all buyers and sellers. Agency disclosure must be documented.
Team Policy
1
Team formation minimum: 6 months tenure + 6 closed transactions
Advisors may not form a team under DBRE until they have been affiliated for at least 6 months and have closed at least 6 transactions at DBRE. Transferring teams: 90-day provisional period applies.
Full policy: DBRE-POL-004 · Real Estate Team Policy · v2.0

Section 12

Safety & Cybersecurity

Advisor safety in the field and the security of firm and client data are operational priorities at DBRE. The policies below represent the minimum standard — Advisors are encouraged to develop personal safety habits that go beyond this baseline.

Field Safety
1
Know your client before any solo showing
  • Require pre-approval or proof of funds before scheduling any showing
  • Verify identity via government-issued ID at first in-person meeting when possible
  • Trust your instincts — if a situation feels unsafe, it is acceptable to reschedule or decline
2
Always have a check-in contact
Before any solo showing with a new or unverified client, notify a colleague of your location, the property address, and your expected return time. Check in after the showing.
Cybersecurity
1
Wire fraud prevention — mandatory client briefing
  • Instruct all clients at the start of every transaction: never wire funds based solely on an email instruction
  • DBRE will never send or change wiring instructions via email without independent verbal confirmation
  • If wire instructions arrive by email, clients must call the title company using a number obtained independently — not from the email
  • Any suspected wire fraud attempt must be reported to the BIC immediately
2
Device and account security
  • Two-factor authentication required on all accounts containing client or transaction data
  • Do not access client files on unsecured public Wi-Fi without a VPN
  • Verify any unusual payment or document request by phone before acting
Open house safety: DBRE-POL-011 · Open House Policy & Procedures · v2.0

Section 14

Independent Contractor Status

All DBRE Advisors are independent contractors, not employees. This relationship has specific legal and tax implications that Advisors are responsible for understanding and managing.

Key IC Principles
1
DBRE does not withhold taxes
DBRE does not withhold federal, state, or local income taxes from commission payments. Advisors are responsible for all self-employment taxes, estimated quarterly payments, and all applicable tax obligations. DBRE recommends all Advisors work with a CPA familiar with real estate IC taxation.
2
Advisors set their own schedules
DBRE does not control the hours, methods, or personal work practices of Advisors. Advisors are free to conduct business as they see fit, within the bounds of DBRE policy, the ICA, NCREC regulations, and NAR standards.
3
Advisors are responsible for their own business expenses
Business expenses — marketing, transportation, professional dues, CE, technology — are the Advisor's own responsibility unless otherwise specified in the ICA.
4
IC status does not limit policy compliance
Independent contractor status does not exempt Advisors from compliance with DBRE firm policies, NCREC regulations, or NAR standards. The ICA incorporates this manual by reference.

Section 16

Policy Violations & Disciplinary Procedures

DBRE takes policy compliance seriously. The firm's policies exist to protect clients, Advisors, and the firm's license and reputation. Violations are addressed promptly, consistently, and in proportion to their severity.

Violation Categories
1
Category 1 — Administrative violations
Failure to upload documents on time, missed CE documentation deadlines, late form submission. Addressed with a written notice and corrective action deadline. Repeated Category 1 violations escalate to Category 2.
2
Category 2 — Policy violations
Showing property without a BRA, unauthorized MLS entries, missing the MLS entry deadline, failure to obtain required written consents, or violations of the cooperative compensation policy. Results in a formal written warning and may include a remediation plan.
3
Category 3 — Serious violations
Fair housing violations, wire fraud facilitation or negligence, misrepresentation, disclosure failures, conduct that exposes the firm to license jeopardy, or repeated Category 2 violations. May result in immediate suspension and ICA termination.
Termination for cause: Category 3 violations may result in immediate termination of the ICA without notice. The BIC retains sole discretion in determining category classification and response.
Process
1
Written notification from the BIC
The Advisor is notified of the alleged violation in writing. The notification includes the policy violated, the facts as understood, and the requested response.
2
5 business days to respond in writing
The Advisor has 5 business days to respond. The response is considered by the BIC before a final determination is made.
3
Written resolution — retained in Advisor's file
The BIC issues a written resolution. All materials related to the violation, response, and resolution are retained in the Advisor's file in the Boardroom.
