02  ·  Step by Step

The Home Buying Process

A clear understanding of the process puts you in control. Here is what to expect from first conversation to closing day.

1

Buyer Consultation

We begin here. This conversation is where we learn your goals, timeline, budget, and priorities. We walk you through the process, answer your questions, and make sure we are the right fit for each other.

2

Pre-Approval

Before searching in earnest, you will want a mortgage pre-approval from a lender. This establishes your buying power, strengthens any offer you make, and keeps your search focused. We can connect you with trusted lending partners if needed.

3

Property Search

Your advisor searches the MLS and off-market channels for properties that match your criteria. We schedule showings, evaluate properties with you, and help you think through each one with clarity.

4

Making an Offer

When you find the right property, your advisor prepares a competitive offer based on market analysis, property condition, and your goals. We negotiate on your behalf to achieve the best possible outcome.

5

Due Diligence Period

Once under contract, the due diligence period begins. This is your window to inspect the property, review the title, confirm financing, and evaluate everything before committing to close. Your advisor coordinates inspections and keeps you informed throughout.

6

Clear to Close

When all conditions are satisfied, your lender issues a clear to close. Your closing attorney prepares final documents, the closing disclosure is issued, and you prepare to sign.

7

Closing Day

You sign, funds are disbursed, the deed is recorded, and the keys are yours. Your advisor is with you every step of the way — including at the closing table.

03  ·  Due Diligence · Financing · Closing

After Your Offer Is Accepted

The period between accepted offer and closing day is where your advisor earns their keep. Here is a closer look at what happens — and what you need to know.

The Due Diligence Period

In North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia, residential contracts include a due diligence period — a defined window of time during which you have the right to investigate the property and terminate the contract for any reason. This period is negotiated as part of your offer.

ItemDetails
What It CoversProperty inspection  ·  Radon  ·  Pest/WDO  ·  Survey  ·  Title review  ·  HOA documents  ·  Appraisal
Due Diligence FeeA negotiated fee paid directly to the seller — non-refundable, but credited at closing if you proceed
Earnest MoneyA good-faith deposit held in escrow — typically refundable if you terminate within due diligence
Key DeadlineWritten notice of termination must be delivered before the due diligence period expires. All repair requests must be submitted and agreed upon prior to the expiration of the due diligence date.
What Happens NextIf you proceed past due diligence, your earnest money becomes at risk — be sure before moving forward

Financing & Appraisal

Your lender will order an appraisal during the due diligence period. If the property appraises below the purchase price, your advisor will discuss your options — renegotiation, making up the difference, or terminating within due diligence. Keep in close contact with your lender throughout this period.

Clear to Close & Closing Day

Once your lender issues a clear to close, your closing attorney will schedule the closing and issue your closing disclosure. Review this document carefully — it shows all final figures. Bring your government-issued ID and certified or wired closing funds.

04  ·  How We Are Paid

Compensation & Transparency

We believe you deserve to understand exactly how your advisor is compensated. There are no surprises, no hidden fees, and no pressure. Here is how it works.

As your buyer's agent, DBRE and your advisor are compensated through a buyer representation agreement, which establishes the terms of our working relationship before we begin. Compensation is typically offered by the seller as part of the transaction — however, you should understand what is agreed to so there are no surprises at the closing table.

ItemDetails
Buyer Representation AgreementA written agreement between you and DBRE that defines the scope of representation, compensation terms, and the duration of our working relationship
How Compensation Is Typically PaidCompensation is negotiated directly between the parties as part of the transaction — not offered through the MLS. Your advisor will discuss the terms before any offer is submitted.
When the Buyer Pays DirectlyIf the seller does not offer sufficient compensation, the difference is paid directly by the buyer — this will always be disclosed and agreed upon in advance.
No Hidden FeesDBRE does not charge hidden fees to buyer clients. All compensation is disclosed in writing before you are obligated to anything.
Your Questions Are WelcomeAsk your advisor about compensation at any time. Transparency is not optional here — it is standard.
Wire Fraud Advisory

Wire fraud targeting real estate transactions is increasingly common. Never wire funds based solely on instructions received by email. Always call your closing attorney directly — using a phone number you have verified independently — to confirm wire instructions before sending any funds.

05  ·  Buyer Discovery

Getting to Know You

The more we understand your situation, the better we can serve you. Your advisor will ask questions like these during our consultation — there are no wrong answers.

Your Goals & Timeline

  • What is motivating your move — is this a lifestyle change, a relocation, an investment, or something else?
  • Do you have a target timeline for being in your new home? Is there a specific date driving that?
  • Have you purchased a home before, or is this your first time?
  • Are you currently renting or do you own a home that needs to sell before you can purchase?

Property & Location

  • What areas or neighborhoods are you drawn to — and is there anywhere you have already ruled out?
  • What does your ideal home look like? Bedrooms, bathrooms, style, outdoor space — paint the picture.
  • Are there any non-negotiables — features or conditions that would take a property off the table immediately?
  • How important is proximity to schools, employment, medical facilities, or other specific amenities?

Financing

  • Have you spoken with a lender yet? Do you have a pre-approval in place?
  • Are you comfortable sharing your approximate price range so we can focus the search effectively?
  • Are you planning a conventional purchase, or are you exploring FHA, VA, or other loan types?
  • Is there anything about your financial situation we should be aware of as we begin — credit, employment changes, etc.?

The Process

  • How would you describe your experience with real estate so far — what has gone well, and what has frustrated you?
  • How do you prefer to communicate — calls, texts, email? How quickly do you typically respond?
  • Are there other people involved in this decision — a partner, family member, or advisor — who should be part of our conversations?
  • Is there anything about the process that feels unclear or that you would like us to walk through today?
