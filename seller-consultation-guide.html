02  ·  Step by Step

The Home Selling Process

A clear understanding of the process removes uncertainty and puts you in control. Here is what to expect from first conversation to closing day.

1

Seller Consultation

We begin here. This conversation is where we learn your goals, timeline, and situation. We discuss market conditions, walk through our approach to pricing and marketing, and make sure we are the right fit for each other.

2

Pricing Strategy

Your advisor prepares a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) based on recent sales, active competition, and property-specific factors. Together, we establish a pricing strategy designed to attract qualified buyers and position your home competitively from day one.

3

Preparation & Staging

First impressions drive buyer decisions. Your advisor will walk through the property and provide guidance on preparation — repairs, decluttering, staging, and curb appeal — so your home is presented at its best before it ever goes live.

4

Professional Photography & Marketing Launch

Professional photography is the foundation of your listing's online presence. Once your home is ready and photography is complete, your advisor activates the full marketing plan — MLS listing, digital marketing, and targeted outreach to qualified buyer pools.

5

Showings & Feedback

Your advisor coordinates all showing requests and communicates feedback after each one. You will never be left wondering how the market is responding. If adjustments are needed, you will know promptly and understand why.

6

Receiving & Negotiating Offers

When offers come in, your advisor presents each one in full and helps you evaluate not just the price, but the terms — due diligence period, earnest money, contingencies, and closing timeline. We negotiate on your behalf to achieve the outcome that best serves your goals.

7

Under Contract: Due Diligence Period

Once under contract, the buyer enters their due diligence period. Your advisor will guide you through inspection negotiations, repair requests, and any appraisal issues — keeping you informed and protecting your position throughout.

8

Closing Day

Once due diligence is complete and the buyer is cleared to close, your closing attorney coordinates the final steps. You sign, proceeds are disbursed, and the transaction is complete. Your advisor is with you every step of the way.

03  ·  Strategy & Market Position

Pricing Your Home

Pricing is the single most consequential decision in the selling process. Get it right from the start — and the market responds. Price too high, and you train buyers to wait.

How We Determine Pricing

Your advisor prepares a Comparative Market Analysis — a data-driven review of recently sold properties, active listings, and expired listings in your area. The CMA accounts for square footage, condition, upgrades, lot characteristics, location nuances, and current market absorption rates. The goal is not the highest possible number — it is the most strategic number, designed to generate strong activity and position you for the best possible outcome.

FactorWhat It Means for Your Listing
Comparable SalesRecently sold properties with similar characteristics in your area establish what buyers have been willing to pay — and what lenders will support through appraisal
Active CompetitionBuyers comparison-shop. Your home competes directly with other active listings, so understanding what else is available shapes how we position yours
Days on MarketHomes that sit accumulate stigma. A well-priced home generates the most activity in the first two weeks. We aim to price so that momentum works in your favor from day one
Property ConditionUpdates, deferred maintenance, and cosmetic presentation all affect buyer perception and offer strength. Your advisor will be honest about how condition factors into positioning
Market ConditionsWhether inventory is low or high, whether buyers are active or cautious — current market conditions inform how aggressively or conservatively we position your price

Appraisal & Lender Considerations

Most buyers finance their purchase. That means a lender-ordered appraisal will be part of the process. If your home appraises below the contract price, it can create friction — the buyer may renegotiate, make up the difference in cash, or terminate within due diligence. Pricing your home in alignment with supportable market value reduces appraisal risk and keeps your transaction on track.

A Note on Overpricing

Sellers sometimes believe starting high leaves room to negotiate down. In practice, overpriced listings generate fewer showings, lower-quality offers, and longer market time — all of which weaken your negotiating position. Your advisor's pricing recommendation is grounded in data, not enthusiasm, and is designed to serve your actual outcome.

04  ·  Offers · Due Diligence · Closing

After Your Home Goes Under Contract

Accepting an offer is the beginning of the next phase — not the finish line. Understanding what happens next is essential to protecting your position through closing.

Evaluating Offers

Price matters — but it is not the only thing that matters. Your advisor will help you evaluate every element of an offer, including the due diligence period length, the due diligence fee, earnest money amount, financing type, closing timeline, and any contingencies. A strong offer is one that closes. We will help you see the full picture.

Offer ElementWhat to Know
Purchase PriceThe offered price — subject to appraisal if the buyer is financing. Cash offers are not subject to appraisal unless the buyer chooses to order one.
Due Diligence PeriodThe window during which the buyer can investigate the property and terminate for any reason. As a seller, shorter is generally better — but the fee and earnest money terms matter equally.
Due Diligence FeePaid directly to you at contract execution — non-refundable. This is the buyer's commitment to taking the property off the market while they investigate.
Earnest MoneyA good-faith deposit held in escrow. If the buyer terminates after the due diligence period expires, you may be entitled to the earnest money. Your advisor will walk you through the specifics.
Financing ContingencyMost financed offers include some form of lender dependency. Understand what happens if the buyer's financing falls through — and at what point in the process.
Closing TimelineDoes the proposed closing date work for your situation? If you need time to find your next home or coordinate a move, timeline is a negotiable term.

The Due Diligence Period — Your Perspective

Once under contract, the buyer has the right to investigate the property and terminate for any reason before the due diligence deadline. During this period, you should expect inspection scheduling, possible repair requests, and an appraisal if the buyer is financing. Your advisor will help you evaluate any repair requests carefully — what is reasonable, what is not, and how to respond in a way that keeps the transaction moving.

ScenarioWhat It Means for You
Buyer Requests RepairsRepair requests are negotiable. Your advisor will help you evaluate each request on its merits — distinguishing between legitimate concerns and overreaches. You are not obligated to agree to everything.
Buyer Terminates in Due DiligenceThe buyer may terminate for any reason before the deadline and receive their earnest money back. The due diligence fee you have already received is yours to keep. Your advisor will assess next steps immediately.
Appraisal Comes In LowIf the property appraises below the contract price, the buyer may renegotiate, pay the difference in cash, or terminate within due diligence. Your advisor will walk you through your options and negotiating position.
Buyer Proceeds Past Due DiligenceOnce due diligence expires, the buyer's earnest money is at risk if they terminate without cause. This is when you have the most certainty that the transaction will close.

Closing Day

Once the buyer receives their clear to close, your closing attorney coordinates the final documents and schedules closing. You will receive a settlement statement in advance — review it carefully, as it shows all final figures including proceeds, payoffs, and closing costs. Bring your government-issued photo ID to closing. Proceeds are typically disbursed the same day or within one business day of closing, depending on the method.

Wire Fraud Advisory

Wire fraud targeting real estate closings affects buyers and sellers alike. Never rely solely on email instructions for any wire transfer. Confirm all wire and disbursement details directly with your closing attorney using a phone number you have independently verified before sending or receiving any funds.

06  ·  Seller Discovery

Getting to Know You

The more we understand your situation, the better we can serve you. Your advisor will ask questions like these during the consultation — there are no wrong answers.

Your Goals & Timeline

  • What is driving your decision to sell — is this a lifestyle change, a relocation, a financial move, or something else?
  • Do you have a target timeline for closing? Is there a specific date or event driving that?
  • Have you sold a home before, or is this your first time navigating the seller side of a transaction?
  • Do you need the proceeds from this sale to purchase your next home, or are those independent of each other?

Your Property

  • How long have you owned the home, and what updates or improvements have been made during that time?
  • Are there any known issues with the property — deferred maintenance, systems in need of repair, or anything a buyer's inspector would likely flag?
  • How would you describe the current condition and presentation of the home — is it move-in ready, or does it need some attention before going to market?
  • Are there any features of the property that you believe are particularly distinctive or that have generated positive attention from neighbors or visitors?

Pricing & Expectations

  • Do you have a number in mind for what you are hoping to net from the sale? Understanding your goals helps us build the right strategy.
  • Have you looked at what similar homes in your area have sold for recently, or would you like us to walk you through that data together?
  • How do you feel about price reductions if the market response calls for one — is that something you are open to discussing if needed?

The Process

  • How do you prefer to communicate — calls, texts, email? How quickly do you typically respond, and how much detail do you like in updates?
  • Are there other people involved in this decision — a co-owner, family member, or advisor — who should be part of our conversations?
  • Have you had any prior experiences selling a home that went particularly well or poorly? Understanding that context helps us serve you better.
  • Is there anything about the process that feels unclear or that you would like us to walk through in detail before we move forward?
